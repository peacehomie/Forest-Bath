The term 'Shinrin-Yoku' (which translates to 'forest bathing' in English) was coined by the Japanese Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry, and Fisheries back in 1982. It involves spending free time wandering in nature, and promotes wellbeing, health, and joy.


My idea with this project is to simulate part of that experience for you to enjoy at home, your office, or whereever else you might be.

Q: What is this place?

A: It's a kind of virtual forest. This website simulates the sound environment one may experience in the forest.



Q: How do I turn the sound on?

A: Once closing this dialog, you'll find the sound toggle button in the top right corner of your screen. Click (or tap) it to turn the sound on and off.



Q: How do I use this website?

A: Once the sound toggle button is set to 'on', you can drag the handles on the volume sliders to change the volume of the different sound elements.



Q: I'm on my phone or tablet, and the sound doesn't work. What to do?

A: Make sure your phone or tablet is not set to 'silent mode'. The sound won't play if it is.



Q: I'm on my computer, and the sound doesn't work. What's wrong?

A: It could be that your current web browser tab is muted. If you right-click on it, you can mute and unmute it from there.



Q: Am I in any way being tracked while using this website?

A: There's no tracking of personal data. I have however implemented GoatCounter on this website in order for me to better understand where its visitors are coming from, as well as which type of device they're using. This information is helpful to me because I'm trying to design this website in a way that makes it accessible to everyone. Since there's no personal data being collected, I've decided not to include a GDPR consent notice when entering this website.



Q: Where do the sounds used on this website come from?

A: I've collected different field recordings from freesound; edited them to fit well together; as well as stripping them of unwanted noise (thanks Ville for your help). Credit goes out to the following freesound users, for their excellent field recordings:
